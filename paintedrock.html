Painted Rock in VR

Project Challenge

How can digital reconstruction preserve and restore fragile cultural heritage?

My Role

Photogrammetry • Texturing • Lighting • Unreal Development • UI Design • Interaction Design

Tools

RealityCapture • Maya • Photoshop • Unreal

Outcome

An immersive VR environment that lets users interactively view the art from its current damaged state to its restored version.

The art itself

The pictograph mural at Painted Rock is currently heavily damaged, but historical photos shows how the wall looked previously. The first intention is to digtally restore the art as faithfully as possible.

On-Site Image Capture

Every surface was photographed from multiple overlapping angles to ensure complete coverage for photogrammetric reconstruction. Whenever possible, image capture was scheduled during overcast conditions to eliminate harsh shadows and produce consistent lighting across the entire rock face.

From cloud to polygons

After image alignment, the software generates a dense point cloud representing millions of measured positions in three-dimensional space. This data becomes the foundation for constructing a highly detailed polygonal mesh that accurately reproduces the original rock surface.

Retopology

The original scan contains millions of polygons—far too dense for real-time rendering. Retopology creates a production-ready mesh that preserves the visual detail of the original scan while reducing the geometry enough for smooth performance in VR and AR applications.

Texturing

Historical photographs served as the primary reference for digitally reconstructing the damaged pictographs. Rather than inventing missing artwork, every restored element was carefully recreated using documented historical evidence while preserving the appearance of the surrounding rock surface.

Scale

Accurate physical measurements recorded on site were used to establish the correct scale of the digital model. Maintaining true dimensions is essential for creating a believable immersive experience and for supporting future educational and research applications.

VR interaction

The completed environment combines optimized geometry, reconstructed textures, lighting, and intuitive VR interaction into a single educational experience. Visitors can freely explore the site, compare the current condition with the historical reconstruction, and appreciate details that are difficult to perceive at the physical location.
