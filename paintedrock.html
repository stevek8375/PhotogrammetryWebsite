Painted Rock in VR

This is a breakdown of my reconstruction of a significant rock art mural in the Carrizo Plain. By referencing historical photos, users can view the restored art in a VR setting.

The art itself

The pictograph mural at Painted Rock is currently heavily damaged, but historical photos shows how the wall looked previously. The first intention is to digtally restore the art as faithfully as possible.

Camera arrays

At the site, photos are taken different angles to get enough visual data to later align the images in 3D space to generate a 3D model. Days of overcast weather was chosen to eliminate shadows for proper capture.

From cloud to polygons

Once the software aligns all of the images, a cloud data set is generated from which a polygonal model is generated.

Retopology

The 3D model goes through a retopology process. This produces a cleaner model and more lightweight, which is essential for VR and AR use.

Texturing

The same process that generated a polygonal model also generates textures. From those textures, the restoration can begin.

Scale

Before everything is assembled for VR use, the model is properly scaled based on measurements taken at the sight.

VR interaction

The optimized model and textures are reassembled for VR use. Lighting and background are added for further immersion. VR interaction is the last stage, which includes navigation and ability to switch the artwork between damaged and restored.
