Hueco Tanks in VR

Project Challenge

How can augmented reality improve access to sites that are difficult to visit or difficult to interpret?

My Role

Photogrammetry • Texturing • Lighting • Unity Development • UI Design • Interaction Design

Tools

RealityCapture • Maya • Photoshop • Unity

Outcome

A mobile AR app that provides accessibility to various rock art sites.

Photogrammetry Reconstruction

The foundation of this project begins with photogrammetry, where hundreds of overlapping photographs are combined to reconstruct an accurate digital replica of the rock surface. Rather than creating a simple 3D model, the goal was to preserve the site's unique geometry, surface textures, and subtle details with enough fidelity to support close examination in an augmented reality experience.

This digital reconstruction becomes the basis for every stage that follows, providing an accurate representation of the original site while allowing it to be explored, enhanced, and experienced in ways that are not possible through traditional photography alone.

Designing for Mobile Exploration

Unlike virtual reality, augmented reality is experienced through the limited field of view of a mobile device. Rather than reconstructing an entire 360° environment, the project focuses on presenting only the information needed for comfortable exploration while maintaining visual clarity and performance.

The interface was designed to encourage users to naturally move around the digital model, viewing the site from different perspectives while preserving the feeling of examining an actual rock face. This approach allows the experience to remain both intuitive and accessible without overwhelming the user with unnecessary visual complexity.

Enhancing Faded Rock Art

Many pictographs have faded over centuries of natural weathering, making some details difficult to distinguish even when standing directly in front of the site. To improve visibility while respecting the original artwork, I developed an enhancement workflow inspired by DStretch, a tool widely used by archaeologists to reveal subtle pigment variations.

Rather than simply increasing image contrast, the enhancement was carefully recreated to emphasize only the original pictographs while preserving the natural appearance of the surrounding rock surface. The result provides a clearer interpretation of the artwork while allowing users to compare the enhanced version directly with its current condition.

Experiencing the Site in AR

The completed application transforms the photogrammetry model into an interactive educational experience. Users can instantly switch between multiple archaeological sites, place the reconstructed rock art within their own environment, and examine it from any angle using augmented reality.

A continuous comparison slider allows visitors to transition seamlessly between the site's present-day appearance and an enhanced interpretation, making weathered pictographs easier to identify while preserving the context of the original rock surface. By combining accessibility, interactivity, and historical interpretation, the application demonstrates how AR can deepen public engagement with cultural heritage.
