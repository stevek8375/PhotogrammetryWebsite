Interactive Climbing App Explorer

Project Challenge

How can interactive 3D visualization help people understand complex movement and spatial relationships?

My Role

Photogrammetry • Animation • Rigging • Unity Development • UI Design • Interaction Design

Tools

RealityCapture • Maya • Unity

Outcome

An interactive prototype demonstrating how immersive visualization can improve route planning.

Selecting the Route

Every climbing route presents its own unique sequence of movements, making the choice of location an important part of the project. I selected a well-known boulder near Bishop, California, because I was already familiar with both the rock formation and one of its established climbing routes. This provided a reliable foundation for comparing the digital reconstruction against real-world climbing references.

By documenting a route with known handholds, footholds, and movement sequences, the project could focus on communicating spatial understanding rather than simply recreating a rock surface.

Capturing Human Movement

The climbing route alone only tells part of the story. To demonstrate how the route is actually climbed, a digital climber was also created using photogrammetry, preserving realistic proportions that would naturally fit within the reconstructed environment.

Combining accurate scans of both the climber and the rock face established a consistent visual scale while providing a believable foundation for studying body positioning and movement throughout the climb.

Preparing for Animation

To accurately demonstrate climbing techniques, the digital climber required a production-quality animation rig capable of supporting complex body mechanics. The custom rig provides intuitive controls for the hands, feet, torso, and limbs, allowing each movement to be refined while maintaining natural body positioning throughout the climb.

Additional animator-friendly controls were incorporated into the rig to improve efficiency when posing the character against the rock surface, making it easier to maintain believable contact points and weight distribution during the animation process.

Visualizing the Climb

Reference footage of experienced climbers was carefully analyzed to understand the sequence of movements required to complete the route. Rather than simply matching individual poses, the animation focuses on accurately conveying body position, balance, and the relationship between each handhold and foothold.

The resulting animation transforms a static climbing guide into a dynamic visualization that allows users to better understand how each movement contributes to successfully progressing through the route.

Interactive Route Exploration

The completed prototype combines photogrammetry, character animation, and interactive controls into a tool designed for exploration rather than passive viewing. Users can freely rotate, zoom, and inspect the climbing route from any angle while following the animated ascent at their own pace.

Interactive playback controls allow the animation to be paused, scrubbed frame by frame, or slowed down to study individual movements in greater detail. Additional overlays identify climbing holds, providing users with multiple ways to understand the route and making the application useful for both experienced climbers and those learning new techniques.
